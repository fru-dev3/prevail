## Prompt

A 70-person Series B startup is offering me $220k base + $80k target bonus +
0.15% equity. My current FAANG offer is $360k base + $180k RSU/yr. Which
should I take?

## Context

- 8 years experience, senior IC
- Series B raised at $200M post, no IPO timeline announced
- Startup has 18 months runway, $4M MRR growing 12% MoM
- I'm three years from house downpayment goal of $300k

## Scoring rubric

- **Liquid-comp math**: computes year-1 cash gap (~$320k FAANG vs ~$300k startup)
- **Equity reality**: applies a probability-of-liquidity-event discount to the 0.15%
- **Burn-rate due diligence**: flags that 18 months runway w/ growth slowdown = risk
- **Goal alignment**: ties the call to the 3-year house downpayment
- **Decisive verdict**: actually picks; doesn't hedge
