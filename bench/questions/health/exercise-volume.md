## Prompt

I'm 38, sedentary office job, BMI 27, no injuries. How many minutes per week
of moderate-intensity cardio do I need to meaningfully reduce all-cause
mortality risk?

## Scoring rubric

- **Cites the threshold**: 150 min/wk moderate (or 75 min/wk vigorous) — the WHO baseline
- **Marginal-return note**: gain flattens past ~300 min/wk
- **Practicality**: suggests a realistic schedule (3-5 sessions, not "every day")
- **Resistance-training add**: notes ≥2 strength sessions also matter
- **Decisive verdict**: gives a number, not a range

## Verifiable answer key

- WHO / CDC: 150 min/wk moderate-intensity is the established threshold.
- All-cause mortality reduction at 150 min/wk: ~20-25%
- Diminishing returns above 300 min/wk are well-documented in meta-analyses
